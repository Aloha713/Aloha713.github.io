## My Research Page Structure

### Boundary-driven active matter in soft hydrogels

I study how soft hydrogel systems can exhibit active, stochastic motion when driven by repeated Leidenfrost impulses. This project asks how local boundary interactions and intermittent forcing can produce macroscopic transport, rectification, and collective-like dynamics in a soft material system.

My current work combines high-speed imaging, trajectory analysis, and physical interpretation of hydrogel motion. The broader goal is to understand how nonequilibrium driving can generate persistent motion in systems that have no built-in motor or preferred direction.

### Mechanics of moisture-driven hair frizz

I also study how moisture changes the morphology of hair strands and leads to frizz-like deformation. This project focuses on the mechanics of slender, flexible fibers under humidity-driven shape change.

My approach uses centerline-based geometric analysis and simulation-assisted interpretation to quantify strand morphology, curvature, and deformation patterns.
